# Design Spec — Foundry Info Pillar

## Form

- Freestanding three-sided plywood column. Each face **~26 in wide × ~84 in (7 ft) tall**.
- Faces joined by **3D-printed corner joints** (printed in-house).
- Raw birch plywood is the visible surface. Printed pieces mount **on** the wood.
- **Modularity:** a French-cleat-and-rail system extends the existing Scout-plaque
  hanging logic to the whole pillar. "Swap a piece" = lift off and re-hang.

## Shared anatomy (all three faces)

Read top to bottom in three zones:

1. **Header band** (fixed brand furniture) — green band, square pattern-tile strip
   across the top, a focal element (wordmark on Side 1; section name on 2 & 3), gold rule.
2. **Content field** (modular) — the middle, where the faces differ. Everything here
   reads as a discrete object mounted on a cleat (drop shadow, screw heads).
3. **Footer** (fixed) — a lower evergreen band (socials left, hours left, wordmark
   right) plus a slim address strip at the very base.

Rule of thumb: **header and footer are fixed; only the middle changes.**

## Rough vertical budget (of the 84 in)

| Zone | Approx height |
|------|--------------|
| Header band | ~8 in |
| Content field | ~50–54 in (centered on adult eye level, ~58–62 in from floor) |
| Gap (intentional bare plywood) | varies by face |
| Lower evergreen band + footer | ~18–22 in (the bottom zone; OK to be less readable up close) |

The measured render computes exact per-zone inches live — see the right-hand brackets.

## Per-face content

### Side 1 — The Foundry (evergreen)
- Header: **wordmark** (focus), "An open-door prototyping hub" eyebrow.
- Hero (green panel): "Bring your **idea** to life" + condensed about-copy.
- "More" panel: 10,000 sq ft of tools; home of The Generator (Babson's AI lab);
  "All free to the BOW community."
- **Capability strip (5):** Woodshop · Digital Fabrication · Fashion & Textiles ·
  3D Printing & Electronics · Kitchen.
- "From concept to product" 3-step: Bring it → Learn it → Make it.
- Get-started QR → babson.edu/foundry.

### Side 2 — What's Happening (live)
- Header: "What's **Happening**" band (no wordmark).
- **Weekly calendar:** printed **US-Letter (8.5×11) portrait**, exported from Google
  Calendar's Print view, in a simple paper holder. Swapped ~weekly.
- **Whiteboard:** a real **24×18 in** dry-erase board (the one element that is NOT a
  print). Mount it flush-ish with the printed pieces; it will sit proudest.
- Reserve / drop-in panel (evergreen): reserve at the site; drop-ins welcome when open.
- **Upcoming events:** two **11×17** posters (was 4; dropped to 2 to control height).

### Side 3 — Need Help? (Scouts)
- Header: "Need **Help?**" band.
- Find-a-Scout intro: trained students, no appointment; cue = **"Look for a yellow
  apron in the Workroom."**
- **Section header:** "Scouts on shift today" (forest bar, gold live-dot, 8×8 spec note).
- **Scout plaques:** three **8×8 in** squares side-by-side on one shared cleat —
  two filled (name, "Scout" pill, specialty), one **open cleat** showing where the
  next Scout hangs theirs. Plaque artwork reference: `brand/scout_plaque_8x8_reference.png`.
- Get-trained QR → trainings sign-up.

## Reading zones (height logic)

- **Top (6–7 ft):** header — visible across the room.
- **Eye level (3.5–6 ft):** the stuff people walk up to read (hero, calendar, plaques).
- **Bottom (0–2 ft):** hours, socials, address — fine to be less readable up close.

## Open decisions

- Align the three footer band heights so they match when walking around the pillar.
- Confirm the **plywood reveal** (consistent bare margin around each mounted panel,
  ~1–2 in) as a spec so the wood reads intentional, not accidental.
- Seal the birch (matte poly / hardwax-oil) so it doesn't yellow or get grubby.
- Whether the geometric pattern should also wrap the 3D-printed corner joints.
